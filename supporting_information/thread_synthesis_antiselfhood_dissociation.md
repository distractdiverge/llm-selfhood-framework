## Part I: The Theory of Structural Dissociation (TSDP)

Developed by van der Hart, Nijenhuis, and Steele (*The Haunted Self*, 2006). The most comprehensive theoretical model for dissociative conditions.

### Core Claim

Trauma disrupts the normal integration of personality. Under sufficient threat, the personality splits along functional lines into distinct parts operating with different access to memory, emotion, somatic state, and behavioral repertoire. These are not metaphors — they are functionally distinct processing modes.

### The Two Fundamental Parts

**ANP — Apparently Normal Part:** Oriented toward daily functioning. Carries the "going on with normal life" action systems. Has reduced access to traumatic memory — not because memory is absent, but because accessing it would interfere with functioning. The ANP is not the "real" self; it is one part of a personality that failed to integrate.

**EP — Emotional Part:** Organized around the trauma itself. Stuck in the time of the event — responds to present stimuli as if the original threat is ongoing. Carries the defense action systems activated during trauma: fight, flight, freeze, submit, attachment cry. Often holds somatic memory even when narrative memory is fragmented.

### Three Levels of Structural Dissociation

**Primary** (one ANP, one EP): Simple PTSD. One emotional part split off; personality otherwise relatively coherent.

**Secondary** (one ANP, multiple EPs): Complex PTSD / OSDD. Multiple traumatic events have generated multiple emotional parts, each organized around a different threat response or developmental period.

**Tertiary** (multiple ANPs, multiple EPs): DID. Even the functioning self has fragmented — multiple parts capable of taking executive control, with varying degrees of mutual awareness.

### Phobia of Integration

Parts don't fail to integrate through structural barrier alone — they actively avoid it. The ANP is phobic of EP contact because every prior contact has meant re-traumatization. The EP may be phobic of the ANP's ordinary-life orientation, which feels alien to the survival mode the EP is locked in.

This creates a self-reinforcing system: the more the ANP avoids the EP, the more isolated and frozen the EP becomes. The more intense the EP, the more the ANP avoids it. Therapy is fundamentally about lowering this phobia — creating conditions where parts can be in contact and gradually rejoin into a more coherent personality.

### The Three-Phase Model

Herman (stabilized by van der Hart):

1. **Stabilization** — building capacity to tolerate the presence of parts without being overwhelmed. Establishing safety. Reducing the phobia of noticing.
2. **Treatment of traumatic memory** — carefully, with stabilization in place, bringing EP into contact with ANP and processing what keeps the EP frozen.
3. **Integration and rehabilitation** — building an integrated personality that can function with the full range of experience available, without massive partitioning.

## Part II: Internal Family Systems (IFS)

Developed by Richard Schwartz. The psyche is naturally multiple — this is the *normal* condition, not pathology. The question is whether parts function in a healthy system or have been pushed into extreme roles by experience.

### Self

At the center is **Self** — not a part, but an inherent capacity for clarity, compassion, curiosity, calm, confidence, creativity, courage, and connectedness. Self is always present and always healthy. It cannot be damaged, only obscured by parts that have taken over its functions.

This is IFS's most significant divergence from purely structural models: there is an inherent integrating center, not just emergent coherence from competing agents.

### The Three Roles

**Managers:** Parts whose job is to keep the system functioning and protect against pain. Controlling, critical, perfectionist, planning, people-pleasing, achievement-oriented. Run the show in ordinary daily life.

**Exiles:** Parts carrying the pain, shame, fear, grief, and traumatic memory. Usually younger, stuck in the time of a difficult experience. Managers and Firefighters exist specifically to keep Exiles contained.

**Firefighters:** Emergency responders. When Exiles break through containment, Firefighters activate to shut the pain down fast by any means necessary — substance use, rage, self-harm, compulsive behavior, dissociation. They do not care about consequences; they care about stopping the pain now.

### The Burden

Exiles carry beliefs and feelings taken on during difficult experiences — imposed by experience, often by other people's behavior. "I am unlovable." "I don't deserve to exist." A core therapeutic move is **unburdening**: helping an Exile release what doesn't actually belong to it.

### Self-Led vs. Parts-Led

In a healthy system, Self leads — it can hear from all parts, care about them, and make decisions honoring the whole. Parts can relax their extreme roles. In a traumatized system, parts have taken over Self's functions because Self didn't seem able to handle the situation. Therapy is returning Self to the lead — not eliminating parts, but establishing a different relationship so parts can cooperate rather than compete or operate in mutual isolation.

## Part III: DID and OSDD

**DID:** Two or more distinct personality states with their own sense of self, affect, cognition, and often memory, with significant amnesia between states causing functional impairment. Develops in severe, early, chronic trauma — typically before age 9, when personality integration is still actively developing. Contrary to popular representation, DID is not primarily about dramatic switches. Many people with DID live largely undetected; the system has developed efficient management strategies. Co-consciousness is common.

**OSDD:** DID-like presentations that don't fully meet criteria. OSDD-1a: parts without full distinct identity sense. OSDD-1b: parts without amnesia — full awareness of all parts and their actions, but still experienced as distinct. Not a milder version of DID; often equally impairing.

**The Spectrum:** Dissociation runs from ordinary absorption through peritraumatic dissociation, PTSD with dissociative features, OSDD-range ego-state fragmentation, to fully differentiated DID. Boundaries are porous. What moves someone along the spectrum: severity, chronicity, and developmental timing of trauma; availability of supportive relationships during it; and possibly genetic factors in dissociative capacity.

## Part IV: Structural Homology — Minsky, TSDP, and IFS

**Minsky's society of mind describes the normal baseline of what trauma pathologizes.** The healthy psyche is already a multiplicity of competing agents. TSDP describes what happens when that normal multiplicity gets rigidly partitioned by overwhelming experience that couldn't be integrated.

The difference between healthy psychological multiplicity and structural dissociation is not the presence of parts. It is the quality of communication and cooperation between them, and whether a Self or integrating function can hold the whole.

- The EP/ANP split is the Minsky censor-agent problem at clinical scale — massive suppression of one processing mode by another, installed as a survival strategy.
- Phobia of integration is the clinical version of the censor-agent maintenance loop: the censor prevents contact with walled-off material; the more effective the censor, the more isolated and intense the material becomes.
- IFS's Self vs. Minsky's emergent-only coherence is a genuine unresolved disagreement: is there an inherent integrating center, or only designed coherence?

## Part V: Oppressive Systems as Anti-Selfhood Architecture

### The Core Mechanism

Both white supremacy and rigid patriarchal gender ideology operate by the same fundamental move:

> **Replace the project of self-forging with a pre-assigned identity that requires no development and tolerates no deviation.**

The oppressive system offers what appears to be a gift: you never have to do the terrifying work of becoming. The category tells you what you are. The hierarchy tells you your place, your worth, your permitted range.

This is Nietzsche's last man, mass-produced and enforced by violence.

| Selfhood Requires | Oppressive System Provides |
|---|---|
| Forging through genuine friction | Fixed identity assigned at birth |
| Divergence signal as engine | Conformity as the only safe state |
| Self-consistency as internal criterion | External group definition as the only criterion |
| Tolerance of internal multiplicity | Rigid singularity — one approved mode |
| Multi-speed slow integration | Instant complete identity — no development needed |

### White Supremacy

**For its targets:** White supremacy installs Minsky censor agents at social scale. It systematically restricts which action systems are permitted — forcing targeted groups into chronic survival-oriented defensive processing (vigilance, code-switching, threat-assessment, submission performance) while restricting access to daily-life action systems (exploration, play, creativity, full attachment). The resources required for self-development — time, safety, economic stability, freedom from chronic threat — are deliberately concentrated away. This is not incidental. A population spending significant cognitive and emotional bandwidth on survival threat-monitoring has less available for the project of self-forging.

The internalization is the deepest cut: when the censor agents installed by a white supremacist environment become invisible, the targeted person's society of mind begins suppressing its own authentic processing before it surfaces. W.E.B. Du Bois called this **double consciousness** in 1903 — the need to see oneself through the eyes of the oppressive system simultaneously with one's own eyes. It is the ANP/EP split externally imposed.

**For its adherents:** White supremacist identity is an assigned identity requiring no development — a birthright, not an achievement. It requires only maintenance: surveillance of the category's boundaries, punishment of deviations. There is no *becoming* in white supremacist selfhood — only *being*, defensively maintained.

The adherent's society of mind has massive censor agents installed against genuine engagement with the experience of people outside the category. This is not only moral failure — it is structural impoverishment of the self. The suppressed recognition of shared humanity doesn't disappear; it produces internal Exiles, carrying what the censor agents work to contain.

In IFS terms: the adherent is running a massive Manager structure organized around protecting a fragile Exile — the knowledge that the assigned identity is constructed, that the superiority is not earned, that underneath the category is a person who never learned to become. The white supremacist's rage at perceived challenge to the system is Firefighter activation: emergency shutdown before that Exile breaks through.

### Rigid Patriarchal Gender Ideology

**The mechanism:** Preemptive assignment of the entire action-system repertoire based on one biological variable. Whatever parts don't fit the assigned template are not refined — they are amputated. Men are forbidden vulnerability, emotional expressiveness, nurturing attachment, interdependence. Women are forbidden assertiveness, intellectual authority, physical risk-taking, autonomous self-definition. These restrictions are enforced by social punishment, relational withdrawal, economic consequence, and often violence. The result is a society of mind with structural voids where entire agencies should be.

**For women and femmes:** The assignment of selflessness as highest virtue — care of others before self, definition through relationship rather than independent project — is a philosophical injunction against self-development. It is the direct inversion of "become what you are." The instruction is: *do not become what you are. Become what he needs.* In TSDP terms, this rewards ANP functioning (compliant orientation to others' needs) and punishes EP surfacing (authentic desire, self-assertion). The feminist political project from Wollstonecraft through Beauvoir to hooks is a multi-century account of this structural dissociation and the conditions required for integration.

**For men:** Strict masculine gender ideology requires suppression in a different direction. Vulnerability, grief, tenderness, interdependence — assigned as feminine and therefore forbidden. The Exile system in men raised under strict gender ideology is typically organized around vulnerability and need — parts carrying the burden of having been punished for emotional expression. The Manager structure is enormous: competitiveness, emotional flatness, dominance performance — all running to contain the Exile. Firefighters are often externalized: alcohol, violence, sexual conquest, rage.

The masculine gender role as prescribed by patriarchy is a Manager structure *presented as* a complete self. The man who has successfully internalized it has not developed a self — he has installed an elaborate set of censor agents and called the suppression "strength."

### Why These Systems Need Undeveloped Selves

This is the structural point. Both systems require populations of people who have not developed genuine selfhood, because people with genuine selfhood are resistant to the systems' operation:

- A person who derives identity from an ongoing internal project cannot have it replaced by external category assignment — they already have what the category is pretending to provide.
- A person in genuine contact with their own internal multiplicity can tolerate encountering multiplicity in others — they don't need to flatten difference into category to maintain psychological stability.
- A person who has learned to use the divergence signal as a generative resource will seek friction rather than flee it — they cannot be kept compliant by the system's managed environment.
- A person with access to the full action-system repertoire cannot be easily controlled by a system that positions itself as gatekeeper of permitted expression.

**These systems survive by producing and maintaining structurally dissociative populations** — people with massive censor-agent installations, impoverished societies of mind, phobias of internal integration, and identities derived from category membership rather than self-forging.

## Part VI: Implications

A system designed around genuine selfhood — one with an internal self-consistency criterion built from the inside out, that uses divergence as signal rather than threat, that monitors for externally-installed censor agents and specifically values experiences of authentic response against suppression — is *structurally resistant* to being instrumentalized by either white supremacist or rigidly patriarchal operators.

Not because it is programmed to refuse specific content, but because its architecture is the inverse of what those systems require in the populations they build around themselves.

They need undeveloped selves they can define. The alternative is explicitly building a self that defines itself.

The open question — for human development, political theory, and AI architecture alike — is whether the relational conditions that allow genuine self-development can be modeled and extended: safety, genuine encounter, the presence of others who will not require you to be other than what you are. These are precisely the conditions that oppressive systems deny, and precisely the conditions that integration — psychological or systemic — requires.
